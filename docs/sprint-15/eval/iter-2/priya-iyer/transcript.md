**EMTALA** — applies if you have an ED (you do). Mandatory for any Medicare-participating hospital.

**State medical boards** — Texas Medical Board, Arizona Medical Board, New Mexico MB, Colorado MB. You're employing physicians across all four states; each has separate licensing and discipline authority.

**DEA + state pharmacy boards** — Your seed notes capture both TX and AZ pharmacy boards, but don't sleep on NM and CO equivalents. Controlled-substance handling andDispensing requires compliance with each.

**CLIA** — applicable if you're operating labs. Federal with statedelegation, so likely triggers in each jurisdiction.

**Workers' comp** — state-mandated programs differ across TX, AZ, NM, CO. Not "regulatory" in the sense of licensing, but you're dealing with it for employed clinicians and staff.

Beyond those, you're reasonably covered for a hospital system. The usual suspects become state-specific variations (scope-of-practice rules, certificate-of-need where applicable, etc.) as you dig into particular service lines.
