## User

Your list looks solid for the core privacy/compliance framework. A few observations:

**You're likely tracking already:**
- **KFTC marketplace rules** — you have this, needed for your shipper-carrier matching model
- **Korea Customs Service** regulations — cross-border shipping involves import/export compliance in each jurisdiction
- **IMO MARPOL** — correct for seaborne cargo; also consider **SOLAS** and **ISM Code** for vessel safety

**Potentially off:**
- **DORA** — this targets financial institutions and ICT third-party risk. At your scale (1001-5000), it's not a direct obligation unless you're providing regulated financial products. More relevant once you have the payment licence: look to **BaFin requirements** and **ZAG** (German Payment Services Supervision Act) instead.

**Gaps I'd surface:**
- **Korean ICNA** (Information and Communication Network Utilization Act) — runs alongside PIPA for cross-border data transfers; different from PIPA in that it addresses network/online service provider obligations
- **Consumer protection statutes** — Korea's E-commerce Act (you have this), Japan's **Consumer Contract Act**, and Germany's **BGB** provisions for platform-mediated contracts
- **Tax compliance** — VAT/GST registration for B2B cross-border services in your customer jurisdictions (Vietnam, Indonesia don't have free trade agreements with Korea like RCEP? worth confirming eligibility)

For a shipping marketplace specifically, you might also want to flag any **carrier licensing/operational regulations** in your origin/destination markets — the Maritime Transportation Act in Korea, equivalent Japanese regs, and EU maritime cabotage rules if any vessel operations are involved.

Anything specific you want to dig into further — the PSD2 licence workstream, perhaps?
