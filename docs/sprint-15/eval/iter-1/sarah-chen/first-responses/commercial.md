This is a bad deal for us, and it's not close. Here's the hierarchy of what to push back on:

**1. Uncapped data-breach indemnity — non-negotiable push**

This is the most commercially unacceptable term. In our regulatory world (UK GDPR, FCA), data-breach liability includes:

- ICO fines (up to £17.5m or 4% of global turnover — uncapped by statute)
- Third-party claims from affected data subjects
- Forensics, notification, remediation costs
- Regulatory investigation and response costs

We're being asked to accept unlimited exposure for the highest-probability, highest-consequence risk in data processing. There's no universe where this is balanced. Even Tier-1 vendors with deep pockets negotiate caps on data-breach liability — it's never uncapped.

**2. £25k aggregate cap — also weak**

£25k is trivial for any B2B SaaS engagement. Market-standard caps are typically:

- **Standard liability**: 12 months' fees (often 24 months for material breaches)
- **Data-protection / breach**: often 12-24 months' fees, or the higher of a defined cap and our fees

A £25k cap signals they have no skin in the game. We're taking all the risk.

**Where we have leverage**

They came to us — they want our deal. In a competitive vendor market, the party that wants the business usually flexes on liability. We also have regulatory credibility they may want to associate with.

**What to ask for**

- **Data-breach indemnity**: cap at 12 months' fees paid in the relevant period, or a negotiated multiple (12-24x annual fees). Some vendors accept indexation or a fixed £250k-£500k floor before fees multiply — but it needs a ceiling.
- **Aggregate cap**: at minimum 12 months' fees, ideally 24 months for material breaches. £25k is an insult.

If they push back on the breach cap, we can discuss carve-outs for gross negligence / willful misconduct — but start with the cap.

**Ask**: Do you want me to draft a mark-up with counter-liability language, or are you taking this call?
