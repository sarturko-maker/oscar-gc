**My prior on this type of renegotiation:**

The two prongs are very different. A 12% uplift with currency linkage is defensible as a pass-through if the supplier's costs are genuinely currency-exposed and the mechanism is transparent. The unilateral SKU discontinuation right is the more worrying ask — it strips continuity out of a six-year relationship and shifts all the re-sourcing risk onto you.

**Opening position I'd recommend:**

1. **On price:** Acknowledge the relationship's value, signal willingness to discuss a reasonable adjustment, but push back on the 12% as a headline. Request a breakdown — what's driving it, what currency exposure they have, and what mechanism they're proposing. If it's a pass-through, it needs guardrails: a specific exchange rate reference (e.g., BoE mid-rate for EUR/GBP), a collar so it's not uncapped, and a frequency (e.g., quarterly true-up). I'd open at something like "we'll consider a capped, indexed adjustment linked to [currency pair] with a floor and ceiling — but not a blanket 12%."

2. **On SKU discontinuation:** Reject unilateral rights. This shifts supply chain risk onto your customers. Counter with a structured process: mutual notice (e.g., 180 days for phase-out of active SKUs, with last-time-buy options), commitment to transition support, and a minimum basket of SKUs that are not subject to discontinuation. Alternatively, propose a re-negotiation trigger — if they want out of a SKU, they bring it to the table and we negotiate a transition.

**Walk-away:**

- On price: The ceiling is probably 6-8% indexed to a transparent mechanism, with a floor/collar. I'd walk if they insist on uncapped pass-through.
- On SKU discontinuation: I'd walk if they won't accept any process beyond 90-day unilateral notice. The minimum is a structured phase-out with transition support.

- Current agreement: Is there an existing price escalation or CPI clause? What does it say on supply continuity?
- The supplier profile: What's their share of your procurement? How easy is it to switch? If they're 40%+ of your magnetic components, your walk-away is weaker.
