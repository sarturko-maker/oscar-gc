## TMB Investigation: Standard Scope & Current Stage

**Where You Likely Are**
The complaint has been deemed **jurisdictional and filed** — TMB has opened a formal investigation. The respondent (the supervising physician) has been notified of the alleged violations and asked to respond. This is the core active-investigation phase.

**What TMB Is Doing**
- Assigning either an **attorney-investigator** (for administrative/scope issues) or **physician-investigator** (for standard-of-care issues) to the case
- Subpoenaing records from the clinic, hospitals, pharmacies — TMB can obtain medical records under HIPAA without patient consent
- Reviewing the delegation agreement, prescriptive authority agreement, chart documentation, and site-visit records
- Timeline target: average 6 months to close; goal within 1 year

**Key production points:**
- Texas requires monthly NP-physician meetings **documented** and chart reviews at least monthly
- The delegating physician must have registered the APRN with TMB within 30 days of the agreement — check whether this was done post-acquisition
- If supervision was "inherited," the question becomes: **who is the legally responsible delegating physician now?** That role does not automatically transfer.

## Defensibility: The Inherited-Protocol Argument

**Strong points:**
1. **Continuity of care** — The protocols existed pre-acquisition; no new supervision model was implemented
2. **Due diligence** — If the APA included representations that the prior owner was in compliance, that's evidence of reliance
3. **No direct change** — You didn't alter the supervision structure — the alleged deficiency predates your ownership

**Gap to watch:**
- TMB will focus on **current** compliance — the question is whether *now* (or during the 3 months you've owned it) the supervision meets Texas standards. "We inherited it" is a *mitigation* argument, not a complete defense.
- If the delegation agreement was never re-executed or re-registered under your ownership, the current supervising physician may technically be in violation
- Texas Occupations Code §157.0512 requires the delegating physician to register the agreement with TMB — this needs to be current

**Actionable defensible posture:**
- Document that the current supervision structure mirrors the prior owner's compliant model
- Show that you've maintained the required monthly chart reviews and meetings post-acquisition
- If you haven't already, verify / re-file the delegation and prescriptive authority registrations with TMB under the current owning entity
